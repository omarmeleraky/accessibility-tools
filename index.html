About Accessibility Tools

Welcome to the Accessibility Tools Catalog - your comprehensive resource for discovering and implementing web and mobile accessibility solutions.

Our Mission

We believe that the digital world should be accessible to everyone, regardless of their abilities. Our mission is to empower developers, designers, and organizations with the right tools to create inclusive digital experiences that work for all users.

By providing a curated collection of accessibility testing and implementation tools, we aim to make it easier for teams to integrate accessibility into their development workflows from day one.

Our Goals

Simplify Discovery: Help teams find the right accessibility tools for their specific needs, whether they're building native mobile apps, web applications, or cross-platform solutions.

Promote Best Practices: Encourage the adoption of accessibility standards and best practices across the industry by making tools more discoverable and easier to evaluate.

Foster Inclusion: Support the creation of digital products that are usable by people with diverse abilities, including those with visual, auditory, motor, and cognitive disabilities.

Accessibility Standards

Our catalog includes tools that support various accessibility standards and guidelines. Understanding these standards is crucial for building truly accessible digital experiences.

WCAG 2.0

Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.0 provides recommendations for making web content more accessible to people with disabilities.

WCAG 2.1

An extension of WCAG 2.0, adding new success criteria to address mobile accessibility, people with low vision, and cognitive disabilities.

Level A

The minimum level of conformance. Addresses the most basic web accessibility features.

Level AA

The recommended level of conformance. Addresses the biggest and most common barriers for disabled users.

Level AAA

The highest level of conformance. Provides the highest level of accessibility for users.

Section 508

U.S. federal law requiring federal agencies to make their electronic information accessible to people with disabilities.
